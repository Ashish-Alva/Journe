  Journe ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your
  information is collected, used, and safeguarded when you use the Journe mobile application.

  1. Information We Collect

  Personal Data
  When you sign in using Google Sign-In, we collect your name, email address, and profile picture provided by Google to
  create and manage your account.

  User-Generated Content
  We collect the text entries, titles, and images you create within the app. This data is stored securely in Google
  Firebase/Cloud Firestore.

  Location Information
  If you grant permission, we collect your precise or approximate location data to "geotag" your journal entries. This
  allows you to view where your memories were created. You can disable this in your device settings at any time.

  Media and Files
  We request access to your device's storage/gallery to allow you to upload images to your journal entries and export
  your entries as PDF files.

  2. How We Use Your Information
  We use the collected data to:
   - Provide and maintain the journaling service.
   - Synchronize your data across devices via your account.
   - Enable location-based features (geotagging).
   - Facilitate the export of data into PDF format.

  3. Third-Party Services
  Journe uses the following third-party services which may collect information used to identify you:
   - Google Play Services
   - Firebase Authentication & Firestore (Data storage and login)
   - Google Sign-In (Authentication)

  4. Data Security
  The security of your data is important to us. Your journal entries are stored using industry-standard encryption
  provided by Google Firebase. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100%
  secure.

  5. Data Deletion
  You have the right to delete your account and all associated data at any time. You can do this within the app settings
  or by contacting us at the email provided below.

  6. Children's Privacy
  Journe does not address anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information
  from children under 13.

  7. Changes to This Privacy Policy
  We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. You are advised to review this page periodically for any changes.
